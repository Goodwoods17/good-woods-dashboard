# Good Woods Dashboard — UI Audit Punch-List

> Generated 2026-05-28 via a multi-agent workflow (9 surface reviewers → adversarial verification of every high/medium finding → synthesis). 66 findings confirmed across 9 surfaces. Audited against `PRODUCT.md` + `DESIGN.md` (canon). This is a design-conformance review, not a correctness check — `tsc`/`lint`/`build` all pass green as of this date.

**Verdict:** The system's foundations are sound (tokens, type scale, the Hitlist and several briefing cards are canon-correct), but two failures recur on nearly every surface: the Ghost-Border Rule is broken almost everywhere (cards are bordered slabs instead of shadow-floated white), and the eight-feet-glance status-dot contract is missing on every list except JobsList. Layered on top are a system-wide 44px touch-target shortfall on the shop-floor/tablet surfaces and four broken/undefined token references that silently kill status color. None of it is structural — it's the same handful of primitives misused at scale, which means a small number of fixes clears most of the board.

## High

**Hitlist health is invisible at a glance — no leading status dot + a hardcoded "at risk" eyebrow that lies** — home+jobs · `Hitlist.tsx:150-186, 209-230, 179` · (S) · breaks PRODUCT.md Principle 1 "status at a glance" and same-day-truth. The morning landing surface shows health only via a right-edge pill, and every row prints a literal "at risk" caption regardless of `deriveHealth`. Add a leading `<StatusDot status={health}>` (mirror JobsList) and drive the eyebrow off `health`/`HEALTH_LABELS`.

**Morning loading skeleton uses bordered cool-gray rows and doesn't mirror the Hitlist** — home+jobs · `page.tsx:97-110` · (S) · breaks Ghost-Border + skeleton-mirrors-final-layout. The first thing Andrew sees each morning is the bordered-table look the system forbids, then visibly jumps shape on hydration. Swap to `bg-surface rounded-xl shadow-resting` with `border-border-faint` dividers at Hitlist row height + a leading dot placeholder.

**Ghost-Border Rule broken system-wide — bordered slab cards instead of shadow-floated white** — all surfaces · `CostsTab/TasksTab/ActivityTab`, estimator (7 containers), `Tile.tsx`/`ReportsView` KpiTile, calendar grid/summary/nav, `ShopColumn`/`WorkUnitCard`, catalog/inventory tables, settings/sops/installer/briefing/login (`Section.tsx` et al.) · (M total) · breaks Ghost-Border Rule / "don't add borders to cards." The single highest-spread issue: `bg-surface border border-border rounded-lg` is the default on roughly every container in the app, reading as the enterprise-SaaS/Bootstrap-admin aesthetic the brand explicitly rejects. Global fix: replace with `rounded-xl shadow-resting`, keep internal separation as `border-border-faint` dividers only. (Several files — JobsList, BriefingCard, DocumentsCard — already do it right, so the pattern exists to copy.)

**Foot-glow occluded on every page** — shared-layout · `AppShell.tsx:18` · (S) · breaks Foot-Glow Rule (the "brand signature," required on every page-level canvas). The shell root paints solid `bg-background` full-height over the body gradient, so the warm foot-glow never shows anywhere in the app. Drop `bg-background` (or set `bg-transparent`) and confirm `<main>` doesn't re-introduce an opaque fill.

**Sidebar fenced off with hard borders — Ghost-Border + admin-shell smell** — shared-layout · `Sidebar.tsx:79,80,135` · (S) · breaks Ghost-Border Rule. `border-r` plus stacked `border-b`/`border-t` chrome dividers are the off-the-shelf admin-shell signifier. Let the white sidebar separate from the canvas via tonal step (optional soft right-edge shadow); replace chrome dividers with spacing or a `border-border-faint` hairline.

**Command-palette input kills its focus ring** — shared-layout · `CommandPalette.tsx:204` · (S) · breaks the absolute "never `outline: none`" focus spec + a11y "focus rings always visible." Remove `focus:outline-none focus:ring-0`; if too heavy, substitute `focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-accent-soft`.

**`button.tsx` is a stock shadcn primitive wired to tokens that don't exist** — design-system-primitives · `button.tsx:6-41` · (M) · breaks the Buttons spec + "Tailwind with our tokens" + anti-reference "off-the-shelf shell." References `bg-primary`/`bg-destructive`/`border-ring`/`--radius-md` — none defined here — and would render broken. Currently dead code. Delete it, or rewrite to spec (ink-pill primary, pill shape, accent-active hover). Until then it must not ship.

**Four undefined/broken status tokens silently kill color** — estimator + catalog · `QuoteSummary.tsx:100,110` (`text-status-success`), `LineItemRow.tsx:48` (`line-through-soft`), `MaterialsTable.tsx:203-218` StaleChip (`status-ontrack`/`status-success`/`status-watch`) · (S) · breaks token canon + status-at-a-glance + WCAG color-not-only. The Net-profit readout, the room-disabled strike-through, and the price-freshness chip all reference names that don't exist, so Tailwind emits nothing and the intended signal vanishes. Map to real tokens: `status-on-track`/`-soft`, `status-at-risk`/`-soft`, `status-blocked`/`-soft`, and standard `line-through`.

**Estimator is a wall of equally-weighted options — no visual lead** — estimator · `EstimatorView.tsx:432-513` · (M) · breaks PRODUCT.md Principle 2 "one primary action" + "don't stack two IAs." Template chip, Project, Rooms, an 11-col line-items table, Cabinet summary, and Quote sidebar all carry identical box treatment with no focal point. Give the active editing section (Line items) resting elevation and collapse/mute completed-or-empty sections into quiet strips so the surface points at one step.

**Status rendered as plain text instead of a badge, bypassing the existing primitive** — crm-contacts · `ContactDetail.tsx:170-172` · (S) · breaks Status Badges spec + status-at-a-glance. Linked-project pipeline status is `replace(/_/g,' ')` bare text — no dot, no fill, no color — while a shared `StatusBadge.tsx` already exists. Render through `StatusBadge`.

**Notes textarea has no associated label (placeholder-only)** — crm-contacts · `ContactForm.tsx:204-212` · (S) · breaks Inputs spec ("placeholder-only labels forbidden") + WCAG "forms always have labels." Every other field uses the `<Field>` helper; Notes sits bare. Wrap it in `<Field label="Notes">`.

**Contacts, Projects, and Margin-by-job are the data-table-with-pills aesthetic, no leading dot** — crm + reports + projects · `ContactsList.tsx:22-106`/`50-56`, `ReportsView.tsx:195-255`/`218-253`, `ProjectsView.tsx:161-247` · (L for contacts/projects, S for reports) · breaks anti-reference #1 + status-at-a-glance. Three multi-column tables with header rows and colored pills, none with a left-edge status dot (Contacts even renders a transparent no-op dot for non-anchors; reports uses heavy `border-b border-border` rows). Lead every row with a `StatusDot` driven by `deriveHealth`; for reports the quick win is the dot + `border-border-faint`; contacts/projects need the larger table-to-row-cards conversion.

**Pipeline value chart uses sage status-color as a fill** — pnl-reports · `ReportsView.tsx:134-137,172-181` · (S) · breaks Charts spec (clay vertical-fade) + "keep the condition axis out of value charts." The `complete` bar is rendered in sage (status-on-track) while others are clay, re-mixing health into a stage chart. Render all bars with the clay vertical-fade (distinguish `complete` via opacity/taupe if needed).

**Work-unit cards carry no condition signal on the eight-feet board** — shop · `WorkUnitCard.tsx:28-62` · (M) · breaks Principle 1 (the board is the surface most explicitly for the eight-feet glance). A unit shows code/description/time but nothing tells you from across the shop if it's stalled or andon-flagged. Add a leading `StatusDot` driven by unit condition (andon, over-time-on-station, or linked-job health).

**Inventory low-stock is color-only on one number** — inventory · `StockTable.tsx:41-73` · (M) · breaks status-at-a-glance + WCAG color-not-only. A below-reorder row is signalled solely by turning the on-hand figure amber — invisible at a glance and to color-blind users on the page whose entire purpose is spotting reorders. Add a leading `StatusDot` (at-risk when `qtyOnHand < reorderPoint`) plus an optional trailing "Reorder" pill.

**Catalog tab nav is underline-tabs instead of the canonical segmented pill** — catalog · `CatalogView.tsx:22-37` · (M) · breaks Segmented Pill spec + Rare-Accent (clay on active tab text+underline). A 2-way exclusive choice is the exact ViewToggle case. Replace with the shared segmented-pill `ViewToggle`.

**LowStockBanner turns a status color into a border** — inventory · `LowStockBanner.tsx:17-23` · (S) · breaks Ghost-Border + alarm-banner anti-reference. `border border-status-at-risk/30` plus amber title pushes toward the Procore/BuilderTrend look. Drop the colored border; keep the soft fill + AlertTriangle so state stays non-color-only.

**Editable grid cells strip the focus ring** — inventory + catalog · `StockTable.tsx:51,70,84,92`, `MaterialsTable.tsx:144,307`, `CrudTable.tsx:63` · (S) · breaks "focus rings always visible" / "never `outline: none`." `focus:outline-none` with only a faint bg tint — barely perceptible on `<select>`, the worst place for it on the dense keyboard-nav CRUD surface. Add `focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-accent-soft`.

**Installer "Done" button fills with sage status-color as a surface** — installer · `InstallCard.tsx:172-178` · (S) · breaks "status colors are pills/dots, never surfaces" + ink-pill button language. `bg-status-on-track text-white` is a saturated-green action button flirting with the safety-color signal. Make it `rounded-full bg-ink-pill text-white hover:bg-accent-active`; the Check icon already carries "done."

**"Call client" is a permanently dead control styled as live** — installer · `InstallCard.tsx:156-164` · (S) · breaks WCAG perceivable-state + native-feel honesty. `href="tel:"` (no number), `aria-disabled` with no value, `preventDefault`, but styled like the working link — a dead end on the truck-phone surface. Wire it to a real number and render only when one exists, or make it a real `<button disabled aria-disabled="true">` with disabled styling.

**Page-level headings use Inter font-semibold instead of Cormorant** — login + sops + estimator · `login/page.tsx:59`, `SopArticle.tsx:18`, and all estimator card heads (`ProjectSection.tsx:18` et al.) · (S) · breaks Typography "Cormorant 500 for all headings/card heads." The serif character is absent below the page title across the estimator and on two primary headings, dropping the editorial serif/sans pairing that is the brand signature. Switch heads to `font-serif text-title/text-headline font-medium`.

**44px touch-target shortfall across every shop-floor/tablet surface** — shared-layout + primitives + crm + estimator + inventory + settings · `Sidebar.tsx:118,146`, `StatusEditor.tsx:79`, `Modal.tsx:53-59`, `ContactDetail`/`ContactForm`/`ContactCombobox`, `LineItemRow.tsx:153-159`/`RoomsPanel.tsx:71-77`, `StockTable`/`MaterialsTable`/`CrudTable`, `RatesSection.tsx:28-39` · (M total) · breaks a11y "44×44px minimum on tablet/phone shop surfaces." Nav links (~32px), the Modal close (~16px), StatusEditor options (~28px), rate inputs (~26px), inline delete buttons (14px icon, hover-only) all fall short — and several delete/edit affordances are `opacity-0 group-hover`, unreachable on touch. Bump interactive rows/buttons to `min-h-[44px]` (or `py-2.5+`) and make hover-revealed controls visible on touch (`@media(hover:hover)` gate or always-on at reduced opacity).

## The one next fix

**Add the leading `StatusDot` to the Hitlist rows (and fix the hardcoded "at risk" eyebrow) — `Hitlist.tsx`.** It's one small, self-contained change on the single surface Andrew opens every morning, and it repairs the product's core contract — status at a glance — exactly where it matters most. The eyebrow fix stops the morning view from actively lying about job health. The win is visible the instant he reloads the page, the primitive (`StatusDot`) and the pattern (JobsList) already exist to copy, and it establishes the template for the same dot fix on Contacts, Projects, Reports, Inventory, and the shop board — so the highest-leverage glance fix also seeds every other one. The Ghost-Border sweep is bigger and more visible overall, but it touches a dozen files at once; for an ADHD owner-operator the right lead is the one move that lands today, on the screen he actually looks at, and makes the rest obvious.
